单词释义

  • 词性：动词
  • 音标：/ɪˈlʊmɪneɪt/
  • 中文释义：照亮；阐明
  • 英文释义：To light up or make clear.
  • 词根词缀起源故事：来自拉丁词"illuminare"，意为“照亮，启发”。"Il-"前缀通常表示强调，而"lumen"意为光。

场景例句

  1. The street lamps illuminate the town square at night. 街灯在晚上照亮了市集广场。
  1. The teacher's explanation helped to illuminate the complex subject for the students. 老师的解释帮助学生明白了复杂的主题。
  1. The documentary aims to illuminate the hidden truths of history. 这部纪录片旨在揭示历史的隐藏真相。

相近词

  1. illumination (名词) - 照明
  1. illuminator (名词) - 照明器；启发者
  1. luminary (名词) - 杰出人物；明星
  1. luminous (形容词) - 发光的；明亮的
  1. luminescence (名词) - 发光；荧光

英文故事

Once, in a small village, a luminary inventor created a luminous toy. Kids were fascinated by its luminescence. It became the town's favorite illuminator during blackouts. People often said, "It's not just a toy; it's our ray of hope!"

曾经，在一个小村庄，一个杰出的发明家创造了一个发光的玩具。孩子们被它的荧光所吸引。在停电时，它成为了镇上最受欢迎的照明器。人们经常说：“这不仅仅是一个玩具，它是我们的希望之光！”

小测验

  1. Which of the following means "to light up"?
    • a. luminescence
    • b. luminary
    • c. illuminate
    • d. hope
  1. What is the origin of the word "illuminate"?
    • a. Greek
    • b. Latin
    • c. French
    • d. English
  1. Which word describes someone who is outstanding in their field?
    • a. luminous
    • b. illumination
    • c. luminary
    • d. illuminator

答案：

  1. c
  1. b
  1. c

单词释义

  • 词性：名词
  • 音标：/jiǒng/
  • 中文释义：一个表示尴尬或者困境的表情；古代指窗户
  • 英文释义：A character used to express embarrassment or a dilemma; in ancient times, it referred to a window.

场景例句

  1. 他发现自己穿反了衣服，脸上露出一个大大的“囧”。 He realized he wore his clothes inside out and had a big "囧" expression on his face.
  1. 当她听到那个尴尬的笑话时，她的反应只是一个“囧”。 Her only reaction to the awkward joke was a "囧".
  1. 我的手机突然关机，真是“囧”啊。 My phone suddenly shut down, what a "囧" moment.

相近词

这部分比较困难，因为"囧"是一个特殊的中文字符，但我们可以考虑一些与“尴尬”情境相关的词：

  1. 尴尬 (名词) - awkwardness
  1. 窘迫 (名词) - embarrassment
  1. 难堪 (形容词) - embarrassed
  1. 笑话 (名词) - joke
  1. 表情 (名词) - expression

英文故事

In a Chinese class, John tried to read a sentence. Accidentally, he pronounced "窗" as "囧". The whole class laughed, and his face turned red. He said, "Oh, what a 囧 moment for me!"

在中文课上，John试图读一个句子。不小心，他把“窗”读成了“囧”。全班都笑了，他的脸变得通红。他说：“哦，对我来说真是一个‘囧’的时刻！”

  1. What did "囧" mean in ancient times?
    • a. Door
    • b. Window
    • c. Wall
    • d. House
  1. Which word is closest in meaning to "囧"?
    • a. 笑话
    • b. 尴尬
    • c. 开心
    • d. 生气

  1. c
